I am a computational chemist with 7+ years of experience and 25+ publications investigating how **electric fields** and **protein dynamics** shape enzyme catalysis. I integrate **machine learning** with **multiscale simulations** (QM/MM, MD) to elucidate fundamental mechanisms of biocatalysis and engineer enzymes with novel functions — and am extending these approaches to **quantum computing** platforms.

Currently a Postdoctoral Scholar at [ETH Zurich](https://chab.ethz.ch/en/), I work on quantum computing pipelines for biological applications, automated reaction network exploration, and rational design of metalloenzymes. Previously at [UCLA](https://www.chemistry.ucla.edu/directory/alexandrova-anastassia-n) (2023–2025), I pioneered computational frameworks that map 3D electric field landscapes in enzyme active sites and used ML to predict enzymatic function from electrostatic signatures. I received my Ph.D. from [Michigan Technological University](https://www.mtu.edu/chemistry/) (2022), studying metalloenzyme mechanisms with [Prof. Christo Z. Christov](https://www.mtu.edu/chemistry/department/faculty/christov/).
